In Aurora, in-situ testing delivers direct subsurface data essential for verifying soil behavior where expansive clays and weathered bedrock dominate. Our field evaluations align with ASTM D1556 and D6938 standards, ensuring reliable compaction control and bearing capacity confirmation. A core offering is the field density test (sand cone method), which provides accurate in-place density measurements critical for engineered fill acceptance in residential and commercial developments across the Denver Basin.

These investigations support foundation design, pavement subgrade verification, and utility trench backfill compliance, particularly in subdivisions and infrastructure projects on the eastern plains. We complement density testing with nuclear gauge correlations and proof rolling observations for comprehensive earthwork quality assurance. Our geotechnical team integrates site-specific results to mitigate risks associated with collapsible soils and shallow groundwater, delivering practical recommendations for Aurora’s growing construction demands.
