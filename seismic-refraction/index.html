Aurora sits on a complex glacial foundation — the city itself straddles the Fox River, and what lies beneath those post-glacial deposits varies wildly from block to block. The 1991 investigation for the Hollywood Casino site showed 15 meters of soft silty clay before hitting competent dolomite, a reminder that two adjacent lots can have completely different subsurface profiles. Seismic tomography (refraction/reflection) maps those transitions using surface-deployed geophone arrays that measure P-wave and S-wave travel times through soil and rock. Our team has run surveys across Kane County where conventional borings alone could not resolve the true depth to bedrock or the lateral extent of buried paleochannels. When the MASW survey gives you the shear-wave velocity profile, seismic tomography fills in the structural geometry — it is the difference between knowing something is there and knowing exactly where.

We resolved a 9-meter bedrock offset on a single commercial lot on the east side — three borings missed it, the seismic line caught it.

Technical details of the service in Aurora

Aurora's population passed 180,000 in 2023, and the pressure on land has pushed development into areas where the Silurian dolomite drops off sharply — sometimes over 20 meters within a single city block. Seismic refraction surveys use a 48- to 72-channel spread with 5-meter geophone spacing to resolve those bedrock steps. The hammer or weight-drop source generates a signal that refracts along the soil-rock interface, and the first-arrival inversion yields a continuous cross-section of layer velocities and thicknesses. For reflection work on deeper targets, we deploy a 24-bit seismograph with 10-Hz vertical geophones and process the data through standard NMO correction and CMP stacking. The result is a velocity model tied to known lithology from nearby SPT borings — a combination that satisfies both the geotechnical and the structural review requirements under IBC Chapter 18.

Local geotechnical conditions in Aurora

The freeze-thaw cycles in northeastern Illinois are relentless — Aurora gets about 100 cm of snow annually, and winter ground temperatures routinely dip below -5°C. That seasonal heave can open hairline fractures in weathered dolomite, accelerating karst development in areas underlain by the Platteville and Galena formations. Seismic tomography identifies those softened or voided zones before they become a construction crisis. On a 2022 warehouse excavation near the BNSF yard, a reflection survey detected a 3-meter-deep cavity in the upper bedrock that standard test pits had completely missed. The contractor adjusted the foundation design and saved an estimated six weeks of delay. Without that pre-excavation image, the first sign of trouble would have been a broken excavator bucket or worse. In karst-prone sections of Aurora, combining seismic data with resistivity profiling provides a second independent check on suspected voids.

Quick answers

How long does a seismic tomography survey take on a typical Aurora commercial lot?

A single refraction line of 115 meters with 24 geophones takes about two hours of field time, including setup and multiple shots. Data processing and a draft tomogram are usually delivered within three business days.

Does seismic refraction work through asphalt or concrete?

Yes, but coupling is critical. On paved surfaces we use a 4.5-kg sledgehammer on a metal strike plate with gypsum paste under each geophone to ensure solid ground contact. The upper pavement layer appears as a high-velocity cap in the tomogram and is stripped during interpretation.

What is the cost range for a seismic tomography survey in Aurora?

Most single-line refraction surveys in the Aurora area fall between US$3,030 and US$5,760, depending on line length, number of shots, and whether reflection processing is added. Multi-line or crosshole programs are priced per linear meter after site walk.

Can seismic tomography detect underground utilities?

Seismic methods are not the primary tool for utility detection — GPR or EM locating is better suited. However, large-diameter concrete storm drains or vaults do appear as velocity anomalies on refraction tomograms, and we flag those features during interpretation.

What is the difference between seismic refraction and MASW for site classification?

Refraction measures P-wave velocity and maps layer geometry; MASW measures shear-wave velocity (Vs) directly and is the standard for ASCE 7 site class (A through F). For a complete site characterization, we often run both on the same geophone spread.
