Local geotechnical conditions in Aurora

Aurora's underlying geology is predominantly Wedron Group glacial till over Silurian dolomite bedrock, but the surface deposits tell a more complicated story. The Fox River has reworked these sediments into terraces of interbedded sand, silt, and organic clay that can vary completely within a single city block. The biggest risk we see on Aurora projects isn't the deep bearing layer—it's the intermediate compressible zones that get missed by borings spaced on a 50-foot grid. A CPT rig pushes through these transitions at 2 cm per second and records every inch. On a warehouse slab near the Burlington Northern tracks, we detected a 3-foot peat pocket that borings on either side had completely bypassed. The IBC 2021 and ASCE 7-22 require site-specific investigation for Seismic Design Category D, which applies to most of Kane County. CPT data also feeds liquefaction triggering analysis using the Robertson and Wride (1998) method—critical for saturated Fox River sands in a seismic event.

Quick answers

How much does a CPT test cost in Aurora, IL?

For most Aurora projects, CPT testing runs between US$190 and US$270 per hour of rig time, which typically covers 40 to 80 linear feet depending on soil conditions. A standard single-location profile to 60 feet in Fox Valley silts and clays usually takes half a day. The total cost depends on depth, number of locations, and whether you need seismic or dissipation testing. We provide a fixed-price quote after reviewing your site plan—no surprises after mobilization.

What depth can a CPT rig reach in Aurora soils?

In typical Aurora stratigraphy—interbedded glacial till, outwash, and lacustrine deposits—we routinely push to 60 or 80 feet. The limiting factor is usually rod friction in dense till or refusal on cobbles and boulders. When we hit the Wedron Group till at depth, tip resistance can spike above 300 tsf and the rig will refuse. In those cases, we can pre-drill through the hard layer and continue pushing, or combine CPT with rotosonic drilling for the deeper profile.

How does CPT compare to standard SPT borings for foundation design?

CPT provides a continuous, nearly undisturbed profile of soil behavior, while SPT gives you data at 5-foot intervals with significant sample disturbance. For compressible layers in Aurora's Fox River deposits—which can be just a few feet thick—that continuity is everything. CPT tip resistance and sleeve friction are also directly usable in bearing capacity and settlement equations without the empirical corrections that SPT N-values require. That said, we often recommend combining both methods: CPT for the continuous profile and SPT for physical samples when you need to run lab tests like consolidation or triaxial.

Do I need CPT testing for a residential addition in Aurora?

It depends on the site. If you're on the east side near the Fox River or in an older neighborhood with undocumented fill, CPT is a smart investment. We've seen 4-foot basement additions in Aurora where the original 1950s footings were on good till, but the addition area was over an old silted-in drainage swale. A single CPT sounding costs a fraction of what a foundation repair runs, and it tells the structural engineer exactly what they're building on. For simpler sites on known till, standard borings may be sufficient—we can advise after a site walk.
